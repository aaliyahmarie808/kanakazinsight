This page showcases some of the best local dishes in Hawaii. This page only lists four different local foods, but there are so many more that Hawaii has to offer! To check out more local foods and dishes, check this link out!





In Hawaii we like to call these, “plate lunches”. These usually consist of white rice, macaroni salad, and some kind of meat or other entree. This image shows a Hawaiian food staple, kalua pig (smoked shredded pork), lomi salmon (salted salmon, tomatoes, green onion), laulau (pork wrapped in taro leaves), and poi (mashed taro mixed with water). There are tons of different types of plate lunches. To read about some of these plate lunches, check out this link!





This noodle dish is what we locals call “saimin”. It’s made from wheat noodles and dashi-based broth. The fix-ins of saimin usually vary from spam, green onion, egg, kamaboko (fish), and char siu (pork). These are perfect for a rainy day, which doesn’t happen too often in Hawaii! Nonetheless, this dish is a fan favorite!





In Hawaii this is called “shave ice”. It’s similar to what Americans call a snow cone, but instead of crushed ice, this is made using shaved ice, hence the name. This ice-based dessert is flavored with different syrups and other sweet ingredients. This image shows a picture of this wonderful treat from Wailoa Store. Located in the heart of Honolulu on O’ahu, this ice cream shop is one of the lesser known shave ice spots. As a local, the shave ice here is definitely one of the best! I recommend getting your shave ice with snow cap, better known as condensed milk.





This dessert has got to be one of my favorite local foods! If you have a sweet tooth, this one’s definitely for you! A malasada, also called, “Portugese fried dough”, is a fried doughnut flavored with lemon zest and covered in cinnamon and white sugar. Although the classic is my favorite, these yummy desserts can also be filled with things like chocolate, custard, and haupia (coconut)! The best place to get these treats is from Leonard’s Bakery, located in Honolulu on O’ahu. They have the main bakery in town but multiple food trucks around the island.
